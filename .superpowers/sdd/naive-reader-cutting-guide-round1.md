# Naive-builder comprehension read — DB40 three-drawer cutting guide (Round 1)

**Reader persona:** homeowner, normal DIY tools (tape, circular/table saw, drill, jigsaw), no prior knowledge of this project, holding only the 11-page printed cutting guide.
**Verdict up front: FAIL** (borderline; the part-breakdown is strong, but hole/groove placement is not self-contained — see grade section).

---

## 1. What I would buy and every operation, in order — with the gaps flagged

### Buying
- **Sheet goods** in four thickness groups: Garnica Duraply 3/4 in (13 parts), 16 mm plywood (12 parts), 12 mm plywood (3 parts), 6.35 mm plywood (1 part). **Gap:** the guide never tells me how many *sheets* to buy or gives a nesting/cut layout. "Buy the sheet goods" plus a parts list, but I have to work out sheet count and yield myself and could easily under-buy.
- **Hardware / mechanism** — the guide says hardware "stays on the assembly manual's kit card," which I don't have. But the guide's own steps require me to already own: the **runner maker's corner template** (step 8), the **runner maker's stepped bit / drawer screws** (steps 6, 13, 14), **stabilizer gear racks, a linkage rod, and left/right pinion sets** (step 10), the **purchased pulls** (step 12), and the **runners** themselves (steps 8–9). None of these are on a buy-list in this document. I can cut parts but cannot assemble a shopping trip from this printout alone.

### Cutting (Step 2) — this part I can do
- Cut all **29 parts** to the wood list. Rule is clear: a size with **≈** is cut to the exact millimeter value beside it; a plain fraction is cut to the tape mark. Keep marked show-face up and grain as marked; label every part; left and right are not interchangeable. **This is complete and followable.** Minor friction below on the mm-in-parentheses.

### Machining, in the document's order
**Step 3 — captured-back groove (Left side, Right side, Cabinet bottom, Rear stretcher):** groove ≈1/4" (6.85 mm) wide × 3/8" deep. The guide gives the position for **only one** of the four parts — the end panel, "3/8" from its rear edge." It then says "the other three positions are in the printed row under each box and in the fabrication packet," but the printed row shows only the end panel. **So I do NOT know where the groove sits on the cabinet bottom or the rear stretcher from this document.** The instruction "cut all four with one setup" implies 3/8" from rear on all four, which contradicts "the other three positions are [elsewhere]." I would have to assume.

**Step 4 — six bottom-to-toe attachment centers on cabinet bottom:** marking only, driven in assembly. **No dimensions are printed** — just a schematic with dots and a "front-left datum." I cannot mark these to any real position; I'd be eyeballing a picture.

**Step 5 — drawer-bottom groove, all 12 drawer-box parts:** ≈1/2" (12 mm) wide × 1/4" deep, 1/2" up from bottom edge, inside face, one fence setting. **Complete and followable.**

**Step 6 — drawer-side screw holes, 6 sides, 4 holes each:** columns 5/16" from each end; first hole 1-1/2" up; second hole 9"/6"/3" up for bottom/middle/top drawer. Values are present. **Two frictions:** (a) it never states which *face* to drill from (the step/countersink would land on the wrong face if I guess wrong); (b) I have to trust that both end columns share the two heights (symmetric), which the diagram implies but doesn't state.

**Step 7 — drawer-back notches + hook holes, 3 backs:** notches 50×13 mm at both lower corners; hook holes 6 mm dia × 10 mm deep, 7 mm in from each side, 24 mm up; measured from lower-left of the rear face; sides get no notches. **Complete and followable.**

**Step 8 — runner pilot holes on 3 drawer-box fronts:** 2 pilots per side, 2.5 mm × 10 mm, 75° set by the purchased template. Fine **if** I own the template. Wording "2 pilot holes per side" is ambiguous (per corner? per face of a corner?), but the template governs, so tolerable.

**Step 9 — runner screw stations, 15 marks per end panel:** "3 rows of 5 stations," 2.5 mm pilots, same on both ends. The station/row numbers are printed in **tiny, barely legible type** and appear to give only **three x-values for five stations per row** ("stations from front edge: 10.02 / 37.09 / 113.97"). **I cannot place all 15 marks from what's printed.** This drives where the drawer runners mount — get it wrong or asymmetric and the drawers bind.

**Step 10 — cut mechanism stock:** gear racks to 560.0 mm, linkage rod to 659.9 mm, per drawer; deburr; keep pinion sets with their drawer. Cut lengths are clear; **buying the stock is not covered here.**

**Step 11 — applied-front clearance holes, 3 drawer-BOX fronts:** 4 through-holes from inside face; columns 233.975 / 701.925 mm from each end; heights 25.4 / 76.2 mm up. Values present, but the note says heights "repeat on the deeper fronts" **and** "per the fabrication rows" — a small contradiction about whether the heights truly repeat.

**Step 12 — pull holes, 3 DECORATIVE fronts:** 5 mm holes, 224 mm apart, centered. Height is given **only for the top front (79.375 mm up)**; "each front's hole height is in its fabrication row." **The middle and bottom fronts' pull heights are not in this document.** Horizontal I can derive (centered on the 1012 mm front); vertical for two of three fronts I cannot.

**Step 13 — cabinet-end screw holes, 9 per end:** step-drilled from outside face. "**EXACT CENTERS (mm) ARE IN THE FABRICATION PACKET.**" A schematic shows a bottom row plus corner pairs, but **no numbers at all.** I cannot drill these correctly from this printout.

**Step 14 — toe-rail screw holes, 4 per rail:** "**EXACT CENTERS (mm) ARE IN THE FABRICATION PACKET.**" Again **no numbers** — a vertical pair near each end, positions unknown.

**Step 15 — edge banding, 19 parts:** 0.5 mm band, all 12 edges of the decorative fronts, never a grooved edge. For the **other 16 banded parts, which specific edges are "scheduled" is never stated** — only "every scheduled edge," with no schedule.

## 2. Wrong-build risks (I follow the document and still build wrong)

1. **Step 3 groove on cabinet bottom + rear stretcher** — with no position given and "one setup" implied, I use 3/8" from rear edge on all four parts. If the true positions differ (the text hints they're "elsewhere"), the back panel won't seat and the box won't pull square. *High impact.*
2. **Step 9 runner screw stations** — I place the 15 marks from illegible/incomplete numbers (3 x-values for 5 stations). Runners end up misplaced or asymmetric left-to-right; **drawers bind or won't align.** *High impact.*
3. **Step 13 cabinet-end 9 screw holes** — no numbers, so I eyeball the schematic. Screws miss the stretcher corners / anchor strip; joints are weak or the carcass racks. *Moderate–high.*
4. **Step 14 toe-rail 4 screw holes** — no numbers; guessed positions misalign where the short returns land. *Moderate.*
5. **Step 12 pull heights** — I guess the middle and bottom pull heights; the three drawer pulls end up at inconsistent heights, a glaring visible defect on the finished piece. *Moderate, cosmetic.*
6. **Step 4 toe centers** — I mark six centers off a dimensionless picture; the toe-kick screws later miss the rails. *Moderate (partly caught in assembly).*
7. **Step 6 drawer-side holes — wrong face** — the step never says which face to drill from; I drill from the inside and put the counterbore on the show face (or mirror left/right wrong). *Moderate.*
8. **Step 11 applied-front heights** — the "repeat / per fabrication rows" contradiction leads me to place the deeper fronts' screws at the wrong height. *Low–moderate.*
9. **Groove width confusion (step 3/5)** — "≈1/4" (6.85 mm)" for a 6.35 mm panel; if I cut a true 1/4" (6.35 mm) or trust the fraction over the mm, the back is too tight. *Low.*
10. **Grain/show-face "decide yourself" (step 1)** — no guidance means I could run grain inconsistently across the three visible fronts. *Low, cosmetic.*

## 3. Smaller frictions

- The **fabrication packet** is referenced as the source of truth for several hole centers, but page 1 labels it "shop copy" and page 2 calls it the machine-shop version — it is **never flagged up front as something the homeowner must also have.** I only discover I'm missing data mid-build (steps 3, 4, 9, 12, 13, 14).
- **Illegibly small** dimension text in the step-9 diagram (and the callout captions generally are tiny on a Letter print).
- **mm-in-parentheses on non-≈ sizes** (cabinet bottom "22-7/8" (580.525 mm)", sides "≈23-1/4" (590.05 mm)") muddies the otherwise clean "≈ = cut to mm, else tape mark" rule; and 580.525 mm ≠ 22-7/8" (581.025 mm). The bottom drawer front height "13-15/16"" has no mm twin while its siblings do.
- **No sheet count / cut layout** for purchasing.
- **Step 8 "2 pilot holes per side"** wording is ambiguous.
- **Step 15** doesn't say which edges are banded on the 16 non-front parts.
- Grey render callout bubbles (esp. step 15) are hard to map to a specific part/edge.

---

## 4. Grade — **FAIL** (borderline vs. Conditional Pass)

**Reasoning.** The part-breakdown half of this guide is genuinely good: I can buy the right sheet groups and cut all 29 parts correctly, and steps 5, 6, 7, 8, 10, 11 give real, followable values. If the document were only a *cutting* guide, it would pass.

But it bills itself as "the buying and cutting path: every cabinet part, groove, hole, and edge band," and it is **not self-contained for holes and grooves.** At least six operations cannot be executed correctly from this printout: the captured-back groove on the cabinet bottom and rear stretcher (step 3), the six toe centers (step 4), the fifteen runner screw stations (step 9, values illegible/incomplete), the pull-hole heights on the middle and bottom fronts (step 12), the nine cabinet-end screw centers (step 13), and the four toe-rail screw centers (step 14). Each of these punts to a "fabrication packet / shop copy" the homeowner isn't given, and the guide doesn't warn at the top that the packet is required.

Facing that, a reader either **stalls** (realizes a second document is needed) or **guesses.** Guessing is the likely path for most homeowners, and guessed runner-screw stations (step 9) and cabinet-end centers (step 13) produce drawers that bind and joints that don't line up — a wrong build, not merely a cosmetic assumption. Because a wrong or incomplete build is the probable outcome for someone holding only this printout, this lands on **FAIL** rather than Conditional Pass. The fix is straightforward: print the exact centers in the step diagrams (as steps 5–8 and 11 already do) instead of deferring steps 3, 4, 9, 12, 13, and 14 to the absent packet, and flag any truly required companion document on page 1.

**Wrong-build risks: 10. Frictions: 7.**

# Naive-Builder Read — DB40 Cutting Guide (Round 2)

**Reader stance:** homeowner, normal DIY tools, no prior knowledge of this project, holding only the 11-page printed `cutting_guide_print.pdf`. I judged only what I could actually determine from the printout.

**Grade: CONDITIONAL PASS** — a careful reader who understands mirror parts and can read the small embedded dimension text gets a correct kit; a naive reader can plausibly mirror a groove wrong or misread a clipped/oversized number.

---

## 1. What I would buy and cut, in order

### Buy
- Four sheet-goods groups, from the wood list on page 2: **Garnica Duraply 3/4"** (13 parts), **16 mm plywood** (12 parts), **12 mm plywood** (3 parts), **6.35 mm plywood** (1 part). 29 parts total.
- The document tells me sheet count and nesting are **my** job (page 2) — I would lay out my own cut plan before buying. This boundary is stated clearly up front. Good.
- The **hardware kit** (stepped bits, runner maker's templates, stabilizer rack/rod stock) I must buy **first** from the assembly manual's kit card — page 2 says so before any drilling step. Also clearly flagged before work starts. Good. This guide does not list hardware quantities, and it says so, so I am not left guessing whether they were omitted by mistake.

### Prep sequence I would follow
1. **Check + mark (step 1):** inspect sheets for the 4 thickness groups; decide and mark each part's show face + grain direction; start the release record (page 11).
2. **Cut 29 parts to the wood list (step 2):** show face up, grain as marked, label each including left/right. Rule: a size written with `≈` is *not* on a tape mark — cut to the exact mm printed (repeated on page 2 and 3).
3. **Captured-back grooves — 4 parts (step 3):** one straight groove ≈1/4" (6.85 mm) wide × 3/8" deep on both cabinet sides, cabinet bottom, and rear stretcher. Positions "up from the lower-left corner as drawn": cabinet bottom 574.175, **left cabinet side 9.525, right cabinet side 574.175**, rear stretcher 94.75 mm. Dry-fit the 6.35 mm back panel.
4. **Toe screw centers — cabinet bottom (step 4):** mark only, 6 centers in two rows. Columns from left 244.475 / 475 / 733.425 mm. Front row 85.725 mm up, rear row (printed value hard to read — see risk 2). No screw may enter the rear groove band.
5. **Drawer-bottom grooves — 12 parts (step 5):** inside face, ≈1/2" (12 mm) wide × 1/4" deep, starting 1/2" up from the bottom edge, one fence setting for all 12.
6. **Drawer-side screw holes (step 6):** 4 step-drilled holes per drawer side (2 near each end), from the outside face; columns 5/16" from each end; first hole 1-1/2" up; second hole 9"/6"/3" up for bottom/middle/top. Test on a scrap coupon first.
7. **Drawer-back notches + hook holes (step 7):** on the 3 backs only — 50 × 13 mm notch at both lower corners; 6 mm dia × 10 mm deep hook holes, 7 mm in from each side, 24 mm up, from the rear face. Sides get no notches.
8. **Drawer-front runner pilots (step 8):** template-seated, 2.5 mm × 10 mm pilots at each front corner, 75° set by the template — not laid out by hand.
9. **Runner screw stations — cabinet ends (step 9):** 15 pilots (3 rows × 5) on the inside face, 2.5 mm, measured from the front edge (10/32/37/261/357 mm) and bottom edge (57.05/386.45/652.45 mm). Depth stop so the bit cannot break the outside face.
10. **Stabilizer rack/rod (step 10):** cut racks to 560.0 mm, linkage rod to 659.9 mm, per drawer; keep pinion sets together.
11. **Drawer-box-front clearance holes (step 11):** 4 through-holes, 5 mm, from the inside face; columns 233.975 mm in from each end; heights 63.5/190.5 (bottom), 44.45/133.35 (middle), 25.4/76.2 (top). Never through the decorative front.
12. **Pull holes — decorative fronts (step 12):** 2 holes, 5 mm, 224 mm apart, left hole 394.5 mm from left edge; heights 177.475/127/79.375 mm for bottom/middle/top; backer board; check against the purchased pull.
13. **Cabinet-end screw holes (step 13):** 9 step-drilled holes per end from the outside face, values "up + from front edge" (bottom row 9.525 up; anchor pair 698.1/746.1 up; front pair 765.175 up; rear pair 765.175 up).
14. **Toe-rail screw holes (step 14):** 4 per rail, pairs 9.525 mm in from each rail end; heights 30.48 / (second value clipped — see risk 4).
15. **Edge-band 19 parts (step 15):** 0.5 mm band per each part's wood-list row; never band a grooved edge; finish the release record.

I could locate a face, a reference, and a size for **every** machining operation except where noted below. Part counts reconcile cleanly: 29 cut parts, 19 banded parts, and the step-15 callout list all agree.

## 2. Wrong-build risks (most important)

1. **Mirror-part groove orientation on the cabinet sides (step 3).** The back-groove diagram draws only the *left* cabinet side, yet gives two different "measure from the lower-left corner as drawn" numbers: left = 9.525 mm, right = 574.175 mm. These differ because the parts are mirrored (the rear edge lands on opposite sides when each is set inside-face-up). A reader who does not reason about mirror orientation has no per-part drawing telling them which physical edge is the rear on the right side, and could measure 574.175 from the wrong corner — putting the right-side groove near the front instead of the rear. Result: the captured back panel won't seat and the case is wrong. This is the single most likely real wrong build. The differing numbers are a clue a *careful* reader can decode, which is why this is conditional rather than fail.

2. **Toe-rail rear-row value exceeds the part (step 4).** The rear-row "up" dimension as I read it (≈984.65 mm) is larger than the cabinet-bottom depth (580.525 mm), so it cannot be marked on the part. The sensible symmetric value would be ≈494.65 mm. Either the printed number is an error, or it is small-print I'm mis-reading; either way, if a reader takes ~984 literally they are stuck or place the row wrong. Needs verification against the actual printout.

3. **Different reference origins on the same part across steps.** The cabinet sides are dimensioned from the "lower-left corner as drawn" (step 3), from the "front edge + bottom edge" (step 9), and "up + from front edge" (step 13); the cabinet bottom uses "lower-left corner" for both groove and toe centers. A reader who re-orients the panel between steps (what counts as "front," "bottom," "left") can land a whole step's holes rotated or mirrored while believing they followed the numbers. No single orientation drawing ties these schemes together.

4. **Clipped toe-rail hole height (step 14).** The diagram's "HOLE HEIGHTS UP FROM BOTTOM" prints as "30.48 / 71..." with the second value running off the box edge. If the second height is unreadable on paper, the reader guesses it — wrong hole height on both toe rails.

5. **"Drawer box — front" vs "drawer front" naming collision.** Step 11 drills clearance holes in the *box* fronts (16 mm ply); step 12 drills pull holes in the *decorative* fronts (Garnica); step 8 pilots the *box* fronts. The names differ by one word. A reader who conflates them could drill 5 mm clearance holes through a decorative front, or pull holes through a box front. Callouts distinguish them, but only if the reader reads carefully.

6. **Show face / grain is the reader's call with no convention given.** Step 1 and the release record push show-face and grain decisions onto the reader and explicitly say "do not infer them from a drawing." For the decorative drawer fronts this can produce a cosmetically wrong build (grain running the unintended way) that still assembles. Flagged as the reader's responsibility, but with zero guidance.

## 3. Smaller frictions

- **Embedded dimension text is tiny.** Every critical hole/groove number lives in ~6 pt all-caps text inside a gray diagram box. On a home printer this is borderline legible and is the practical root of risks 2 and 4.
- **Diagram text overflows its box** in at least steps 9 and 14 (header/heights run to or past the frame).
- **Mixed inches + millimeters** with the `≈`-means-exact-mm rule stated once on page 2; easy to forget by step 11.
- **"2 near each end" vs "4 screw holes" (step 6)** and "2 pilot holes per side" (step 8) require the reader to reconcile per-end vs per-part counts.
- **Isometric images are decorative** — solid gray blobs with callout bubbles, not dimensioned — so the reader leans entirely on the text tables for every measurement.
- **Cross-document links** (assembly manual, fabrication packet, review sheet) are dead on paper, but their roles and the fact that hardware/nesting live elsewhere are stated before work begins, so this is expected, not a defect.

---

## 4. Grade justification

**CONDITIONAL PASS.** Structurally the guide is strong: part counts reconcile, every machining operation has an identifiable part/face/reference/size, and the two big scope boundaries (buy the hardware kit first; do your own nesting) are flagged clearly before any cutting. But correctness depends on the reader supplying two things the document doesn't hand them: (a) the mirror-orientation reasoning needed to place the right-hand cabinet-side groove and to keep "front/bottom/lower-left" consistent across steps that use different origins, and (b) the ability to read small, in one case oversized/off-part, in one case clipped dimension text. A careful reader clears both and gets a correct kit; a naive reader can mirror a groove wrong or misplace a toe row while believing they followed the guide. That is the definition of conditional, not a likely-wrong FAIL.

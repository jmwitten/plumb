# Naive-Builder Read — DB40 Cutting Guide (Round 4)

**Reviewer stance:** Homeowner with normal DIY skills (tape, circular/table saw, drill, jigsaw), no prior knowledge of this project, holding only the 11-page printed `cutting_guide_print.pdf`.

**Grade: CONDITIONAL PASS** — 7 wrong-build risks, 7 frictions.

---

## 1. What I would actually do, in order

### Buy first
Page 2's BEFORE-YOU-START box sends me to the assembly manual's kit card to buy the hardware kit *first* — because the drilling/cutting steps below use "the makers' templates, the matching stepped bit, and the stabilizer rack and rod stock from that kit." I don't have that document, so I note that I literally cannot start several steps (6, 8, 9, 10, 13, 14) without it. This boundary is stated plainly. Good.

Then I buy sheet goods. Page 2 tells me sheet count and nesting are **my** job — plan my own layout from the wood list. Four material groups:
- Garnica Duraply 3/4 in — 13 parts
- 16 mm plywood — 12 parts
- 12 mm plywood — 3 parts
- 6.35 mm plywood — 1 part (captured back, 39-1/4" × 29-3/4")

Largest parts are the 40" toe-kicks and the ≈39-13/16" (1012 mm) drawer fronts, so everything nests on standard 48"×96" sheets. I can do this, but it is real planning work with no help from the guide.

### Cut (Steps 1–2)
Check sheets flat/undamaged/labeled. **Mark each part's show face and grain — my choice** (page 11 release record confirms: "do not infer them from a drawing"). Cut all 29 parts to the page-2 wood list, show face up, grain as marked, and label each as it comes off the saw because "left and right pieces are not interchangeable." Where a size shows `≈`, I cut to the exact mm printed, not the tape fraction.

### Machining, part by part

**Step 3 — captured-back groove, 4 parts** (left side, right side, cabinet bottom, rear stretcher). One groove each, ≈1/4" (6.85 mm) wide × 3/8" deep, same blade/depth. Positions "up as drawn" from each part's lower-left: cabinet bottom **574.175**, left cabinet side **9.525**, rear stretcher **94.75**, right cabinet side **574.175**. Check: "every groove hugs the edge nearest the cabinet's back." Dry-fit the back panel before glue.

**Step 4 — cabinet bottom, 6 toe-screw centers**, plan view, from front-left (lower-left): three per row from left **244.475 / 488.95 / 733.425**; front row **85.725** up, rear row **564.65** up. Black band (back groove) = no screws. Mark only.

**Step 5 — drawer-bottom groove, all 12 box parts**, inside face, from front-bottom corner: ≈1/2" (12 mm) wide × 1/4" deep, starting 1/2" up. One fence setting.

**Step 6 — drawer-side screw holes, 6 sides**, from front-bottom corner, outside face: columns 5/16" from each end; first hole 1-1/2" up; second hole **9"** (bottom), **6"** (middle), **3"** (top). Test the stepped bit on a clamped offcut, reject if it splits.

**Step 7 — drawer backs (3), corner notches + hook holes**, from lower-left: notches **50 × 13 mm** at both lower corners; hook holes **6 mm dia × 10 mm deep, 7 mm in from each side, 24 mm up**. Sides get no notches.

**Step 8 — drawer-box fronts (3), corner pilots**: seat the runner maker's template, bore 2 pilots per side, **2.5 mm × 10 mm deep**, template sets 75°.

**Step 9 — cabinet ends (2), runner screw stations**, inside face, from front-bottom (front edge = banded edge): stations from front edge **10 / 32 / 37 / 261 / 357 mm**; row heights **57.05 / 396.45 / 652.45 mm**; 2.5 mm pilots, depth-stopped so they don't break through.

**Step 10 — per drawer**: cut stabilizer racks to **560.0 mm**, linkage rod to **659.9 mm**; deburr; keep left/right pinion sets with their drawer.

**Step 11 — drawer-box fronts (3), 4 clearance holes each**, inside face, from lower-left, 5 mm through: columns **233.975 mm in from each end**; heights up — bottom **63.5 / 190.5**, middle **44.45 / 133.35**, top **25.4 / 76.2**. Never through the decorative front.

**Step 12 — decorative fronts (3), 2 pull holes each**, from lower-left, 5 mm through, **224 mm apart, left hole 394.5 mm from left edge**; height — bottom **177.475**, middle **127**, top **79.375**. Backer board behind show face; check spacing against the bought pull.

**Step 13 — cabinet ends (2), 9 screw holes each**, outside face, from bottom-front corner (up / from-front): bottom row **9.525 up — 50 / 290.512 / 531.025**; anchor pair **698.1 / 748.1 up — 565.15**; front pair **765.175 up — 25 / 75**; rear pair **765.175 up — 504.425 / 554.425**.

**Step 14 — toe rails (front + rear), 4 holes each**, from left-bottom: pairs **9.525 mm in from each end**; heights **30.48 / 71.12 mm**.

**Step 15 — band the 19 parts** (each part's banded edges are named on its wood-list row), 0.5 mm band, never on a grooved edge, trim, keep labels, sign the release record.

I can determine part / face / datum / size for essentially every step. The failure points are below.

## 2. Wrong-build risks (most important)

**WBR-1 — Left cabinet-side back groove looks mis-positioned relative to the other three.** The four Step-3 positions don't mutually reconcile. Sides are 590.05 mm deep; the bottom is 580.525 mm deep (sides run 9.525 mm deeper). For the back panel to seat flat, all four grooves must be the same distance from the cabinet's back plane. The cabinet bottom groove (574.175 of 580.525) is 6.35 mm from *its* back edge, which lands ~15.875 mm from the deeper side back edge — matching the **right** side (574.175 of 590.05 = 15.875 from back). The **left** side value (9.525) puts its groove ~9.5 mm from back, roughly **6.35 mm out of line with the right side.** Cutting exactly as printed, both grooves still "hug the edge nearest the back," so the page-3 sanity check *passes for all four* and hides the error. A 6.35 mm stagger in a 6.85 mm-wide groove cannot be absorbed — the panel binds or won't seat. The mandatory dry-fit ("before anything is glued") is the only thing that catches it, and only as a "why won't this go in" rework, not a clear diagnosis. This is the single biggest wrong-build trap.

**WBR-2 — Two nearly identical part names invite drilling the wrong front.** "Bottom/Middle/Top **drawer front**" (Garnica, decorative, band all 4 edges, pull holes in Step 12) vs "…**drawer box — front**" (16 mm ply, groove + 75° pilots + 4 clearance holes in Steps 5/8/11). The names differ only by the words "box —." If I grab the wrong one, I put 5 mm clearance holes or 75° pilots into a finished decorative front, or pull holes into a box front. Steps 11/12 do warn ("never drill through the decorative applied front"), which is the only guardrail; the naming does the opposite of helping.

**WBR-3 — "Inside face" vs "outside face" is never fixed to a mark.** Step 1 has me mark only *show face* and *grain*. But Step 9 drills runner pilots on the **inside** face and Step 13 drills cabinet screws on the **outside** face of the same panels — two pages apart. Nothing tells me which physical face is inside, and nothing tells me to mark it. If I drill Steps 9 and 13 on the same face, or lose track between pages, the panel is scrap. It's recoverable only by noticing the 2.5 mm pilots and calling that face "inside" — deduction the document never asks for.

**WBR-4 — The two cabinet sides are identical blanks that must become a mirror pair, but the guide repeatedly says "identical on both ends."** Left and right sides have the same dimensions and the same single banded edge; the only thing making one "left" is which face ends up inside. Steps 9 and 13 both say "both ends use exactly the same printed values." A literal reader drills both panels the same and installs them as twins, not mirrors — putting one drawer-runner set on the outside of the assembled cabinet. Correct build depends on my *understanding* that "same values, from the front-banded edge of each panel's inside face" yields a mirror pair — an inference, not an instruction.

**WBR-5 — Inconsistent `≈` marking on drawer-box side heights.** Back/front read "≈10" (253.5 mm)" (cut to 253.5), but left/right sides read "10" (253.5 mm)" with **no `≈`**. By the page-2 rule, "no `≈`" means "cut to the tape mark" = 254 mm. So I'd cut sides at 254 and fronts/backs at 253.5 → a 0.5 mm height mismatch on the banded top edge of every drawer. Same pattern to watch on the middle/top boxes.

**WBR-6 — "Measure from lower-left corner" is not physically defined per part.** Which corner is lower-left depends on how each blank is oriented, which the guide leaves to me. It's anchored only by the banded front edge plus notes like "hugs the back." For symmetric hole rows this is harmless, but for the side grooves (WBR-1) and any asymmetric layout it's where an orientation flip silently mirrors the whole pattern.

**WBR-7 — Cabinet bottom must hold one orientation across Steps 3 and 4.** The back groove (Step 3) and the six toe-screw centers (Step 4) are both "from lower-left," but on different reference corners of the same board across two pages. Flip the board between operations and the screw rows land at the front / the groove lands at the front. The banded front edge is the only keep-me-honest feature, and the guide never says "keep the front edge toward you for both steps."

## 3. Smaller frictions

**F-1 — Constant inch↔mm switching.** Steps 3/5/6 mix fractions and mm; Steps 4/7/8/9/11/12/13/14 are pure mm. Toggling units step to step is fatigue-error territory even though each value is explicit.

**F-2 — Dark, low-contrast diagrams.** The 3D renders (Steps 1, 2, 10, 15) are near-black on white. Step 15's callout puts **19 tiny numbered circles on a dark cabinet** — I cannot reliably read which number sits on which part, so the banding map is effectively text-only.

**F-3 — Callout boxes are dense all-caps blocks.** Every real dimension lives in small all-caps text inside the gray box; the picture beside it carries almost no information. Slow to parse, easy to skip a line.

**F-4 — No overall cabinet dimensions or part-relationship summary.** I never see "the cabinet is X×Y×Z" or how bottom/sides/stretchers assemble. I reverse-engineered that the toe-kick 40" = cabinet outer width and that sides run 9.525 mm deeper than the bottom — the document states neither, so I can't cross-check my parts against an intended whole.

**F-5 — Per-drawer height values must be matched by hand.** Steps 6, 11, and 12 each give three height sets keyed to bottom/middle/top. Grabbing the wrong row (e.g., middle heights on the top drawer) is an easy, unguarded slip.

**F-6 — Step 6 count wording.** Prose says "4 screw holes in each of the 6 drawer sides"; the callout says "2 near each end." I have to reconcile that 2 per end × 2 ends = 4. Minor but a momentary stall.

**F-7 — Proprietary "maker" vocabulary with no picture.** "Runner maker's corner template," "drawer-screw maker's stepped bit," "stabilizer gear racks," "linkage rod," "pinion sets" appear with no illustration. Correctly flagged to the kit/assembly manual, but within this sheet I'm trusting names I've never seen.

---

## 4. Grade justification — CONDITIONAL PASS

The guide is genuinely thorough: explicit datums, exact mm for every `≈`, and real safeguards (dry-fit before glue, offcut test before drilling, depth stops, "hugs the back" check, "never drill the decorative front"). Its stated boundaries — buy the kit first, nesting is yours, show-face/grain are yours — are all flagged **before** work starts, as required.

It falls short of a clean PASS because a careful homeowner reaches a correct kit **only by supplying judgment the document doesn't give**: catching the left-side groove mismatch at dry-fit and re-cutting (WBR-1), deducing inside vs outside faces with no mark to rely on (WBR-3), realizing the two "identical" sides must become a mirror pair (WBR-4), keeping the two near-identical "front" parts straight (WBR-2), and noticing the stray missing `≈` (WBR-5). None of these is likely to produce a glued-up disaster — the mandatory dry-fit and inside/outside pilots are observable catches — so this isn't a FAIL. But because a strictly literal build mis-cuts at least one part (left side groove) and can mirror a cabinet end, the outcome is correct *only if the reader makes the right assumptions*. That is exactly CONDITIONAL PASS.

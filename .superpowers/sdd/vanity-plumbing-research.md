# Double-sink, four-drawer floating vanity — plumbing and drawer research

**Research date:** 2026-07-14

**Scope:** a photo-inspired, nominal 72 in floating vanity with two undermount
lavatories and four drawer fronts (two per 36 in bay). This is a research and
release-gate brief, not a permit drawing or a claim that the plumbing or wall
mount has been engineered.

## Executive decision

The robust cabinet strategy is **not** four ordinary full-depth boxes. Each
36 in sink bay should be its own serviceable system:

- an upper full-extension drawer with a modeled U-shaped/notched void around
  the basin, drain, tailpiece, trap, and required hand/tool access;
- a lower, shorter full-extension drawer in front of a continuous rear
  plumbing/service zone;
- no fixed shelf or stretcher crossing either bay's service opening; and
- independently trapped left and right lavatories, with both drawers removable
  without disturbing plumbing.

That arrangement is supported by current Blum, Hettich, and Hafele sink-drawer
systems. The exact U width/depth and lower-drawer length are **not catalog
constants**. They must be derived from the selected drain/trap fittings and the
as-built waste/supply rough-in.

## 1. Selected sink: Kohler Caxton K-20000

### Manufacturer requirements and published facts

The current US Kohler specification identifies K-20000 as an undermount,
vitreous-china rectangular basin with overflow and no faucet holes. It includes
basin clamps `1193643` and requires undermount cutout template `1281904-7`,
which is not included. Kohler explicitly requires the countertop fabricator to
use the product template or a current Kohler-provided copy; the drawing alone
is not a cutting template.

| Fact | Published value |
|---|---:|
| Overall plan envelope | 20-1/4 x 15-11/16 in (514 x 398 mm) |
| Overall depth below rim | 7-5/16 in (186 mm) |
| Bowl opening/area | 17-5/8 x 13-1/8 in (448 x 334 mm) |
| Bowl depth | 5-5/16 in (135 mm) |
| Drain hole | 1-3/4 in (44 mm) |
| Tailpiece shown | 1-1/4 in OD |
| Drain center in plan | approximately centered: 10-1/8 in across the width and 7-13/16 in across the depth |

Primary source: [Kohler K-20000 current specification](https://resources.kohler.com/webassets/kpna/catalog/pdf/en/K-20000_spec_US-CA_Kohler_en.pdf).

### Design assumptions, not manufacturer requirements

- Provisional sink centers are 18 and 54 in from the left end of a 72 in top.
  That gives 36 in center-to-center and 18 in from each outer end.
- The top is provisionally 22 in deep. This is **not released** with the current
  faucet candidate: a nominal 9 in wall-to-drain center puts the 15-11/16 in
  sink envelope only about 1-3/16 in from the finished wall. That may be too
  tight for the basin rim, clamps, wall finish, and fabricator's required stone
  web. The actual Kohler cutout template and fabricator must settle it.
- The countertop thickness, reveal, clip locations, adhesive/sealant system,
  edge distances, and stone reinforcement are deliberately unclaimed until the
  countertop material and fabricator are selected.

## 2. Wall-mount faucet rough-in candidate

The researched candidate is Kohler Purist trim `K-T14414-4` with required
in-wall valve `K-410-K`.

### Manufacturer requirements and published facts

- `K-T14414-4` is the nominal 9 in (229 mm) wall-to-drain-center version.
  Kohler's drawing also gives an 8-1/4 in minimum and 9-1/2 in maximum reach
  envelope; the engine should preserve both the nominal selection and the
  actual drawing envelope rather than collapse them to one literal.
- The bottom of the spout must be 1-1/2 to 6 in (38 to 152 mm) above the
  fixture rim.
- The trim sheet calls for three 1-1/2 in cutouts. The valve sheet calls for
  three 1-5/8 in cutouts; the current installation guide/template must resolve
  that interface before wall work.
- The K-410-K rough valve uses 5/8 in OD hot and cold supplies, with the two
  handles on 4 in centers each side of the spout center.
- The valve sheet gives finished-wall roughing ranges of 1-5/8 to 2-5/8 in and
  2-1/2 to 3-1/2 in at the indicated bodies. Kohler warns that there is **no
  deep roughing-in kit** and says to follow the dimensions precisely.

Primary sources:
[Kohler K-T14414-4 specification](https://resources.kohler.com/webassets/kpna/catalog/pdf/en/K-T14414-4_spec.pdf) and
[Kohler K-410-K valve specification](https://resources.kohler.com/webassets/kpna/catalog/pdf/en/K-410-K_spec.pdf).

### Design consequences and unresolved choice

The faucet reach, sink depth, backsplash/wall build-up, and top depth are one
coupled layout. For a 22 in top, the nominal 9 in candidate produces a tight
rear basin/clamp zone. The engine must not independently center the sink and
then "place a faucet above it." Release requires one of:

1. fabricator approval of the K-20000 template at the 9 in drain center with
   the final wall/top/backsplash build-up;
2. a different certified faucet reach that moves the drain center forward; or
3. a deeper top and coordinated longer-reach faucet.

The wall model must also reserve two valve rough bodies, supplies, fastening,
and an installation/service volume. Wall finish thickness is an input, not a
default.

## 3. NYC and model-code plumbing constraints

These are code requirements to encode as jurisdiction-scoped constraints or
release gates, not instructions to perform regulated plumbing work without a
Licensed Master Plumber. The official 2024 IPC is a useful model-code
cross-check; New York City's adopted amendments govern an NYC installation.

### Fixture placement and service access

- NYC PC 405.2 requires access for cleaning the fixture and surrounding area.
- PC 405.3.1 requires a lavatory center at least 15 in from a side wall,
  partition, vanity, or other obstruction; adjacent fixtures must be at least
  30 in center-to-center; and the room must provide at least 21 in clear in
  front. The provisional 18/54 in sink centers satisfy the first two dimensions
  within a 72 in top, but front clearance is a site/room gate.
- PC 405.8 limits slip joints to the trap inlet, trap outlet, and trap seal. If
  those joints are concealed, access must be through a panel or utility space
  at least 12 in in its smallest dimension, or another approved arrangement.
  The vanity therefore needs a verified service opening after both drawers in a
  bay are removed; "the drawer comes out" is not enough unless the remaining
  opening and tool path are actually measured.

Source: [NYC 2022 Plumbing Code, Chapter 4](https://www.nyc.gov/assets/buildings/codes-pdf/cons_codes_2022/2022PC_Chapter4_FixturesWBwm.pdf).

The 2024 IPC carries the same 12 in concealed-slip-joint access rule at
Section 405.9 and requires a lavatory waste outlet at least 1-1/4 in at
Section 419.3. The section-number difference is itself a reason to keep code
citations in a jurisdiction/version profile rather than in generic prose.

Source: [ICC 2024 IPC, Chapter 4](https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IPC2024V2.0/chapter-4-fixtures-faucets-and-fixture-fittings).

### Drain, trap, and vent

- NYC PC 1002.1 requires each plumbing fixture to be separately trapped,
  prohibits double trapping, and limits the fixture-outlet-to-trap-weir
  vertical distance to 48 in and the fixture-outlet-to-trap-inlet horizontal
  distance to 30 in.
  Model two independent traps; do not treat two side-by-side lavatories as one
  combination-sink exception.
- NYC PC 1002.3 prohibits S-traps. Early geometry may use two ordinary vented
  P-trap-shaped study envelopes, but neither the fitting family nor its exact
  dimensions are released until the plumber selects listed components and the
  vent connection is verified.
- PC 1002.4 requires a 2 to 4 in liquid trap seal. PC 1002.5 requires the trap
  to be at least the Table 709.1 size and no larger than the drainage pipe it
  serves.
- NYC Table 709.1 lists a lavatory as 1 drainage fixture unit with a 1-1/4 in
  minimum trap; the official 2024 IPC table publishes the same values.
- PC 909.1 limits trap-to-vent developed length. At 1/4 in/ft slope the table
  gives 5 ft for a 1-1/4 in trap and 6 ft for a 1-1/2 in trap. This is a
  building-rough-in/plumber check, not something cabinet geometry can infer
  from the visible wall stub.

Primary sources:
[NYC 2022 Plumbing Code, Chapter 10](https://www.nyc.gov/assets/buildings/codes-pdf/cons_codes_2022/2022PC_Chapter10_TrapsWBwm.pdf),
[Chapter 7](https://www.nyc.gov/assets/buildings/codes-pdf/cons_codes_2022/2022PC_Chapter7_SanitaryDrainageWBwm.pdf), and
[Chapter 9](https://www.nyc.gov/assets/buildings/codes-pdf/cons_codes_2022/2022PC_Chapter9_VentsWBwm.pdf).

### Critical jurisdiction difference

The official 2024 IPC Section 1002.1 uses a **24 in** maximum vertical distance
from fixture outlet to trap weir, while NYC's current adopted text above uses
**48 in**; both use 30 in horizontally. The engine must not label either number
as a universal plumbing fact. For an NYC release, cite and apply the NYC
profile; during early cross-jurisdiction design, 24 in may be used only as an
explicitly conservative study limit.

Sources:
[ICC 2024 IPC Section 1002.1](https://codes.iccsafe.org/s/IPC2024P1/chapter-10-traps-interceptors-and-separators/IPC2024P1-Ch10-Sec1002.1) and
[ICC 2024 IPC Table 709.1](https://codes.iccsafe.org/s/IPC2024V2.0/chapter-7-sanitary-drainage/IPC2024V2.0-Ch07-Sec709.1).

## 4. Drawer strategies supported by official systems

### A. U-shaped upper drawer — preferred starting strategy

Blum's official sink-cabinet guidance says its U-shaped pull-out uses the space
to the left and right of the sink, can be built from standard LEGRABOX,
MERIVOBOX, or TANDEMBOX components, and can also be a wood drawer on MOVENTO.
It is a full-extension solution. This is strong evidence for the topology, but
Blum does **not** provide a universal plumbing-void size.

Sources:
[Blum sink cabinet overview](https://www.blum.com/us/en/products/cabinet-applications/sinkunit/overview/) and
[Blum TANDEMBOX sink pull-out memo](https://d2.blum.com/services/BEC003/box1551-fl-349_fl_dok_bau_%24sen_%24aof_%24v1.pdf).
Blum's current wraparound instructions also show that a physical U box needs
inside profiles/dividers and their fixings, not just two narrow floor wings.
That construction check directly informed the study's front floor bridge and
two inner return walls; the exact joinery remains gated with the runner system.
Source: [Blum wraparound drawer instructions](https://www.blum.com/file/cme105788_ma_dok_bus?country=us&language=en).

Hettich likewise publishes an AvanTech YOU drawer with a bottom cutout and says
other configurations are possible. Its Banio frame provides a useful bound on
commercially supported adjustability: depending on the drawer configuration,
the siphon cutout can be 99–435 mm wide and 45–283 mm deep; it is intended for
600–1200 mm cabinet widths and nominal drawer lengths up to 500 mm. Those are
hardware applicability limits, not permission to choose the smallest notch.

Sources:
[Hettich AvanTech YOU application](https://www.hettich.com/fileadmin/Media_Center/Catalogue/AvanTech_YOU_Division_2023_EN.pdf) and
[Hettich Banio frame](https://shop.hettich.com/us_EN/Drawer-systems/Interior-organisation/Solutions-for-drawers-pot-and-pan-drawers-with-bottom-cutout/Banio%2C-frame%2C-white/p/9153385).

### B. Local bottom notch/siphon surround — valid only after component selection

Hettich sells square and round metal surrounds specifically for a U-bend
bottom cutout. The round form is supported from 300 mm nominal drawer length,
is recommended from 400 mm cabinet width, and adds 67 mm plus the bottom cover
in height. Hafele's Matrix Box P catalog likewise offers a molded drawer insert
with a siphon cutout (article 547.66.705), published as 350 x 540 x 76 mm.

These products prove that a bounded local notch can be buildable. They do not
prove it clears this basin, drain, trap, wall stub, and the arc required to
assemble or remove them.

Sources:
[Hettich metal U-bend surround](https://shop.hettich.com/us_EN/Drawer-systems/Interior-organisation/Solutions-for-drawers-pot-and-pan-drawers-with-bottom-cutout/Metal-surround-for-U-bend-cutout---round%2C-silver/p/9204282) and
[Hafele Matrix Box P siphon-cutout insert](https://www.hafele.pl/pl/product/wk-ad-do-szuflad-z-wyci-ciem-na-syfon/P-00866761/).

### C. Shortened lower drawer plus rear service chase — preferred companion

Commercial runner systems support deliberately shorter drawers. For example,
Hafele Matrix Box S full-extension soft-close sets are offered in nominal
lengths starting at 270 mm and continuing through 450 mm, and Blum MOVENTO is
offered in multiple nominal lengths with full extension and integrated
BLUMOTION soft close. This supports a lower drawer that stops in front of the
trap/wall connection instead of wrapping around it.

The rear-chase depth remains a project-derived dimension. A provisional
15 in/381 mm lower drawer in a roughly 21–22 in body is suitable for layout
studies only; it is not released until the real fittings, connection arcs,
service hand clearance, and drawer travel have been collision-checked.

The current DV72 analytic path produces a much shorter lower envelope than
that early 15 in assumption. It must **not** inherit MOVENTO by vocabulary:
Blum's current US guide starts at a 305 mm / 12 in drawer length and publishes
corresponding minimum inside depths. Until the plumbing is re-coordinated to
retain that depth or a documented short-depth system is selected, the lower
runner family remains explicitly unselected; only the full-extension and
soft-close performance requirement survives.

Sources:
[Hafele Matrix Box S](https://www.hafele.co.uk/en/product/matrix-box-s-drawer-set-35-kg-84-mm-high/P-01298679/) and
[Blum MOVENTO](https://www.blum.com/us/en/products/runnersystems/movento/programme/) and
[Blum MOVENTO US planning guide](https://d2.blum.com/services/BEC003/movento_ep_dok_bus_%24sen-us_%24aof_%24v7.pdf).

### D. Manufacturer-integrated space-saving drain — known alternative, not v1

Geberit ONE uses a coordinated basin and space-saving drainage system that
removes the conventional trap cutout from the drawer. This is the most
storage-efficient researched topology because it moves the waste path into a
manufacturer-designed rear zone instead of wrapping a generic drawer around a
generic trap. It is also a tightly coupled fixture/plumbing choice: its benefit
cannot be transferred to the selected Kohler basin by copying the shape or
using an unverified bottle trap.

Source: [Geberit ONE washplace and drawer system](https://www.geberit.com/en/insights/new-design-possibilities-at-the-washplace/).

The engine should eventually support a `space_saving_manufacturer_system`
strategy whose fixture, drain, trap, wall connection, service access, and
jurisdiction approval arrive as one selected adapter. For this DV72 increment,
the robust default remains the ordinary, independently trapped P-trap topology
plus derived U/short drawers; the Geberit path is recorded so a future owner can
trade catalog lock-in for more storage without redesigning the base language.

## 5. Floating wall-mount patterns from current manufacturer guides

Two current Kohler wall-hung vanity guides establish a useful comparison
pattern, while remaining product-specific instructions rather than a capacity
claim for DV72:

- Prepare verified stud framing or continuous 2x6 backing before the wall is
  closed. One guide calls for at least two studs, including framing at both
  ends of the rear rail, and added 2x6 support where that pattern is absent.
- Route the planned waste and supplies while the wall is open; plumbing and
  backing are one coordination problem because neither may remove the other's
  required space.
- Use a full-width temporary support while positioning a wall-hung body. Set
  the case level and plumb before drilling or installing the heavy top.
- Drill the rear rail at its ends and at every available stud/backing location,
  then fasten through the rail into the verified structure. One Kohler guide
  specifies 5/16 in lag bolts with a 500 lb load-bearing rating and derives
  length from backing, wallboard, and rail thickness. Those values are evidence
  of the connection facts an engine must carry; they are **not** DV72 fastener
  selection or capacity.
- Install the top only after the wall-hung case is secure and level. Include the
  top, two basins, water, drawers, contents, and foreseeable service loads in
  the project-specific load case.

Primary sources:
[Kohler wall-hung vanity installation guide 1216526-2](https://techcomm.kohler.com/techcomm/pdf/1216526-2.pdf) and
[Kohler vanity installation guide 1527256-2](https://resources.kohler.com/webassets/kpna/catalog/pdf/en/1527256-2.pdf).

The reusable engine consequence is a `WallMountAssembly` with explicit wall
survey, backing, continuous rail, fastener axes, pilot holes, embedment,
edge/end distances, temporary-support method, level/plumb checks, top-load
state, and commissioning evidence. A generic screw SKU and three cylinders in
a rendering cannot establish the connection.

## 6. Geometry the engine must keep separate

For each sink bay, model at least these distinct envelopes:

1. **Fixture envelope:** basin body, rim/clamp zone, drain opening, overflow,
   countertop cutout/template identity.
2. **Operating envelope:** open/close water controls and usable sink volume.
3. **Assembly envelope:** room to offer up the basin, install/torque clamps,
   install the drain, and connect/disconnect the trap.
4. **Plumbing envelope:** selected drain body, tailpiece, trap, fixture drain,
   wall stub, supply/valve parts, and their joint locations.
5. **Service envelope:** the code-required/approved access opening plus hand
   and tool paths to every slip joint and replaceable component.
6. **Drawer-motion envelope:** closed box, full-extension sweep, sag/tolerance,
   and removal path for upper and lower drawers.

Passing only a static solid-intersection test is insufficient. A trap can fit
behind a closed drawer yet be impossible to assemble, tighten, inspect, or
remove.

## 7. Blocking release gates

Keep the vanity at **NOT RELEASED / UNKNOWN**, and suppress fabrication- and
installation-ready language, until all of the following are true:

1. **Fixture lock:** exact sink SKU/color is selected; current Kohler template
   `1281904-7` is obtained; its revision/digest is recorded.
2. **Countertop lock:** material, thickness, reveal, edge treatment, clamp and
   adhesive/sealant system are selected; fabricator approves both cutouts,
   webs, clip access, and the finished-wall relationship.
3. **Faucet lock:** exact trim and valve are selected; current installation
   guide resolves reach, hole diameters, wall-depth range, and finish build-up.
4. **Site survey:** finished wall plane, vanity/top elevations, stud/backing
   layout, both waste centers/elevations, vent arrangement, supplies, and room
   clearances are measured.
5. **Plumbing lock:** two drain assemblies, two traps, fixture-drain fittings,
   wall adapters, and service shutoff strategy are selected from official
   dimensions. A Licensed Master Plumber approves trap/vent/rough-in design.
6. **Drawer derivation:** U/notch dimensions and lower drawer length derive from
   those selected components with explicit construction tolerance; no catalog
   clearance is copied as a project clearance.
7. **Dynamic verification:** closed, full-extension, removal, plumbing-assembly,
   and service-tool envelopes are collision-free in both bays. With drawers
   removed, each concealed slip-joint service path satisfies PC 405.8 or has an
   explicitly approved alternative.
8. **Field commissioning:** wall mount is independently structurally released;
   plumbing is pressure/leak tested before drawers are installed; drawer fronts
   and pulls are aligned only after fixture/top loading is present.

## Platform implication

The reusable pack surface should carry manufacturer facts as selected adapters,
not hand-entered prose or project literals: a `FixtureAdapter`,
`FaucetRoughIn`, `PlumbingAssembly`, `DrawerAvoidanceStrategy`, and explicit
assembly/service/motion envelopes. Manufacturer CAD/BIM can accelerate
visualization and envelope extraction, but release checks should use audited
analytic dimensions, template identity, source URL, revision/digest, units,
coordinate frame, and license provenance.

# Naive-builder read — Cutting Guide, round 5

Reader profile: homeowner with a tape measure, circular/table saw, drill, jigsaw. No prior knowledge of the project. Single document: `cutting_guide_print.pdf` (11 pages). I judged only what the printout tells me.

Round focus per the request: whether the dimensioned machining drawings (ordinate dims, top row = across, left column = up, leaders to ticks) read correctly at print size.

---

## 1. Walk-through of the preparation work

**Page 1 (cover) / Page 2 (Before You Start + wood list + tools).** The intent is clear: buy sheet goods, cut every blank, machine grooves/holes, band edges, label parts, and sign the release record on the last page. The ≈ convention is stated up front ("a size written with ≈ is not on a tape mark, so cut to the exact millimeter value printed beside it"). Good. Buy-the-kit-first and nest-your-own-sheets boundaries are stated. Fine.

Counting the wood list against its own headers:
- Garnica 3/4 in — **13 parts** → I count 13 rows. OK.
- 16 mm plywood — **12 parts** → I count 12 rows. OK.
- 12 mm plywood — **3 parts** → I count only **2 rows**: "Bottom drawer box — bottom — 37-5/16" × 20-3/16"" and "Middle drawer box — bottom — 37-5/16" × 20-3/16"". **There is no "Top drawer box — bottom" row.**
- 6.35 mm plywood — **1 part** → 1 row. OK.

So the group headers sum to 29 (13+12+3+1) and Step 2 says "Cut all 29 parts," but only **28 parts are actually enumerated**. The top drawer clearly needs a bottom panel the same as the other two drawers (Step 5 grooves all 12 drawer sides/fronts/backs for their bottoms; three drawers = three bottoms). I cannot cut a part that has no row, no size, and no material assignment. This is a blocker: I either stop, confused by "3 parts" showing two, or I build a top drawer with no bottom. **This is the single most serious defect in the document.**

**Step 1 / Step 2 (Page 3).** Check sheets, mark show face and grain, start the release record, then cut all parts show-face-up, label each, mark the banded edge. The warning about ≈ and "Left and right pieces are not interchangeable" is well placed. Fine — except I can only cut 28 of the claimed 29 (see above).

**Step 3 — captured-back groove (Page 4).** Four grooved parts. Groove ≈1/4" (6.85 mm) wide × 3/8" deep. The drawing shows a small rectangle with **"9.525"** labeled across the top AND **"9.525"** up the left. The per-part positions are listed separately:
- CABINET BOTTOM: 574.175
- LEFT CABINET SIDE: 9.525
- REAR STRETCHER: 94.75
- RIGHT CABINET SIDE: 574.175

Problem for a first-timer: a groove is a single line and needs one locating dimension plus a direction (is it a vertical groove located *across* from the datum, or a horizontal groove located *up*?). The drawing shows the value in **both** directions, and the per-part list gives a **single bare number** for each part without saying whether it is "across" or "up." The only disambiguation is the caption sentence "Every groove hugs the edge nearest the cabinet's back: if yours lands near the opposite edge, the blank is flipped." That forces me to reason out the geometry rather than read it off the drawing. Left side 9.525 vs Right side 574.175 (a mirror pair) especially invites a wrong-edge groove.

**Step 4 — 6 bottom-to-toe screw centers, cabinet bottom (Page 4).** Datum front-left corner (lower-left), banded front edge at drawing bottom.
- Across (top row): **244.475, 488.95, 733.425** (3 columns).
- Up (left column): **574.175, 564.65, 85.725** (3 values, but only 2 screw rows).
The caption tells me the black band at 574.175 is the back groove — "no screw may enter it." So 85.725 is the front screw row and 564.65 the rear screw row; 574.175 is the groove, not screws. The rear row (564.65) and the groove (574.175) are only ~9.5 mm apart. Marking-only step, and the caption warns me, so manageable — but the two near-back values are close enough to swap if the leader lines are read casually.

**Step 5 — drawer-bottom groove (Page 5).** One value, **"13"**, with "STARTS 1/2" UP FROM BOTTOM EDGE," groove ≈1/2" (12 mm) wide × 1/4" deep, all 12 drawer parts identical. Clear and unambiguous.

**Step 6 — drawer-side screw holes (Page 5).** Datum front-bottom corner.
- Across (top row): **8** and **525** (two columns, 5/16" in from each end of a ~533 mm side).
- Up (left column): **76.2** and **38.1**.
First hole 38.1 (1-1/2" up); second hole varies by drawer: BOTTOM 9" up, MIDDLE 6" up, TOP 3" up. The drawing's 76.2 is the *top* drawer's second hole (3" = 76.2). The caption says "the deeper drawers' second hole sits higher, per the printed rows." So for the bottom and middle drawers I must use the text values (9"/6"), not the 76.2 on the drawing. Workable because the caption flags it, but the drawing itself shows one drawer's height with no label saying "top drawer only."

**Step 7 — drawer-back notches and hook holes (Page 6).** Datum lower-left corner.
- Across (top row): **7, 50, 885.9, 928.9**.
- Up (left column): **24, 13**.
Notches 50 × 13 mm at both lower corners (so 50 and 885.9 are the notch inner edges on a ~935.9 part); hook holes 6 mm dia × 10 mm deep, 7 mm in from each side (7 and 928.9), 24 mm up. Reads correctly once I pair "7 mm in from each side" with the 7/928.9 ticks and "notches" with 50/885.9. The 7 and 50 ticks sit close on the left; the 885.9 and 928.9 close on the right — legible here but tight.

**Step 8 — front-corner pilot holes (Page 6).** Template-set 75° holes, 2.5 mm × 10 mm. No layout dims needed. Fine.

**Step 9 — runner screw stations, 15 marks (Page 7).** Datum front-bottom of side blank, inside face up, mirror pair.
- Across (top row): **10, 32, 37, 261, 357** (5 columns).
- Up (left column): **652.45, 396.45, 57.05** (3 rows). 15 marks total.
The rows are cleanly separated. But two of the across columns — **32 and 37 — are only 5 mm apart.** At print size those two labels and their leader lines nearly overlap, and I cannot confidently tell which dot is 32 and which is 37. The column spacing is also very irregular (10 → 32 → 37 → 261 → 357), which removes the "evenly spaced, so I can infer it" fallback.

**Step 10 (Page 7).** Cut stabilizer racks to 560.0 mm, rod to 659.9 mm. From the kit. Fine.

**Step 11 — applied-front screw holes (Page 8).** Datum lower-left.
- Across (top row): **233.975, 701.925** (233.975 in from each end).
- Up (left column): **76.2, 25.4** (the top/shallowest drawer, per "The shallowest box front is shown").
Per-drawer heights in text: BOTTOM 63.5/190.5, MIDDLE 44.45/133.35, TOP 25.4/76.2. Same pattern as Step 6 — drawing shows one drawer, text carries the rest. Well-captioned.

**Step 12 — pull holes on the decorative fronts (Page 8).** Datum lower-left.
- Across (top row): **394.5, 618.5** (224 mm apart, left hole 394.5 from left edge).
- Up (left column): **79.375** (top drawer; text gives BOTTOM 177.475, MIDDLE 127, TOP 79.375).
Clear, and it tells me to check spacing against the purchased pull first.

**Step 13 — cabinet-end screw holes, 9 holes (Page 9).** Datum bottom-front corner, outside face up, mirror pair.
- Across (top row): **25, 50, 75, 290.512, 504.425, 531.025, 565.15** (7 values).
- Up (left column): **765.175, 748.1, 698.1, 9.525** (4 values).
This is the densest drawing: 9 holes located against 7 across-ticks and 4 up-ticks — not a clean grid, so each dot depends entirely on its own two leader lines being readable. **25/50/75 are clustered within 50 mm, and 504.425/531.025/565.15 within ~61 mm; on the up axis 765.175/748.1/698.1 sit within ~67 mm.** With three near-parallel leaders crowded into each of those bands, I cannot be sure at print size which dot owns which value. This is the drawing I'd be most likely to mis-drill on.

**Step 14 — toe-rail screw holes (Page 9).** Across **9.525, 1006.475**; up **71.12, 30.48**. 4 holes, both rails alike. Clean and unambiguous.

**Step 15 (Page 10).** Band the 19 parts named on their wood-list rows, never a grooved edge, finish the release record. I independently counted 19 banded parts from the wood list (7 Garnica + 12 in the 16 mm group) — consistent.

**Page 11 — release record.** Four panel-lot fields matching the four thickness groups, plus finish-faces/grain, labels, approval. Consistent with the four groups.

## 2. Wrong-build risks (I could build wrong while believing I followed the document)

1. **Missing "Top drawer box — bottom."** The 12 mm group header says "3 parts" but lists only 2 (bottom and middle drawer bottoms); the 29-part total does not match the 28 enumerated rows. Result: a top drawer with no bottom panel, or a builder blocked with no size/material for the third bottom. **Blocker / must-fix.**
2. **Step 3 captured-back groove orientation.** The drawing shows 9.525 both across and up, and the per-part values are bare single numbers with no "across/up" label; only prose ("hugs the back edge") disambiguates. On the mirror-pair sides (left 9.525 vs right 574.175) I could cut the groove on the wrong edge/orientation and still believe I matched the printed number.
3. **Step 13 nine-hole cluster.** Clustered ticks (25/50/75; 504.425/531.025/565.15; 765.175/748.1/698.1) with crowded leaders make it easy to assign a dot to the wrong neighboring value at print size, drilling a structural cabinet-end hole in the wrong place.
4. **Step 9 columns 32 vs 37.** 5 mm apart; the two labels/leaders can be swapped, misplacing runner-screw pilots.
5. **Step 4 rear screw row vs back groove.** 564.65 (rear screws) and 574.175 (groove, no screws) sit ~9.5 mm apart; a casual leader read could put screws into the groove band the caption forbids.

## 3. Smaller frictions

1. Datum is named three different ways — "front-bottom corner," "bottom-front corner," "front-left corner" — though each is parenthetically pinned to "(LOWER-LEFT)," so it resolves.
2. Steps 6, 11, 12 draw only the top (shallowest) drawer's heights; the other drawers' values live only in the caption text. Well-flagged, but the drawing carries an un-annotated single-drawer value.
3. "MEASURE FROM: CUT-LIST LOWER-LEFT (LOWER-LEFT)" (Step 3) doubles "lower-left" redundantly.
4. Mixed units on one part (e.g., cabinet side "30-1/2" × ≈23-1/4" (590.05 mm)") — one dimension is a tape mark, the other an exact-mm ≈ value; I must not cut 23-1/4" (590.55) when 590.05 is meant. The ≈ rule covers this, but it demands attention on every mixed row.
5. Odd-precision values (290.512, 233.975, 574.175) are fine to mark but read as machine output on a hand-tool document.

## 4. Grade

**CONDITIONAL PASS.**

Most of the guide is genuinely buildable by a first-timer: the ≈ convention, datum-per-step, banded-edge naming, template-set angled holes, and the release gate all work, and the majority of the dimensioned drawings (Steps 5, 7, 11, 12, 14) read cleanly off the page. The dimensioned-drawing format itself is sound.

It is held back to CONDITIONAL by one true defect and a legibility cluster:
- **Must-fix:** the 12 mm group claims 3 parts but lists 2 — the Top drawer box bottom is absent, and the 29-vs-28 count is internally inconsistent. A cutting guide's core job is a complete part list, so this is the load-bearing failure.
- **Should-fix:** Step 3's groove drawing needs a single, direction-labeled locating dimension per part rather than a bare number in two directions; and Steps 13 and 9 need their clustered ticks/labels spread or leadered so a print-size reader can't misassign a dot.

Fix the missing bottom and de-cluster Steps 9/13, and this reaches a clean PASS.

## 0. The owner directive, and why the order must be derived

Owner (Joel, 2026-07-11, verbatim intent): *"with complex installations we
should break it up into steps. So showing step 1: screws step 2: glue (if
that's the right order). that would make it easier."*

Two things are in that sentence. First, the deliverable: build documents that
present the assembly as a sequence of steps, each with its own view — the
reader-facing half of this design (§5). Second — and the owner's own
parenthetical "(if that's the right order)" says it — the order itself is a
fact someone can get wrong: for the caddy his example order is backwards. The
shipped caddy spec's own assumptions say glue-and-cure comes BEFORE the side
screws (the clamped bond registers the rails; screws follow —
`details/armchair_caddy.spec.yaml`, task-glue-caddy-report.md §Caddy re-spec).
That is not a correction of the owner; it is the design's motivating evidence:
**a hand-authored sequence is exactly the kind of load-bearing construction
fact that rots** (the D6 station arc, R40, is the same failure one level down),
so the sequence must be DERIVED from the model where the model knows it, and
visibly DECLARED with provenance where it doesn't — never hand-typed prose.

This design also pays down the largest standing honesty debt on the board:
**18 named `UNKNOWN — install-order dependent` axis-2 verdicts** blocking three
delivered documents (caddy 8, sit_reach_frame 8, platform 2 —
task-install-axes-report.md §Per-detail verdicts, progress.md SESSION UPDATES
7-8), each one worded "before a construction process graph exists (axis 3)."
This is that graph. The adversarial review probed all three details live; the
frame's eight forced a real scope decision (§3.4 — its blockers are mutually
symmetric, so no flat sequence can clear them; the real build benches each side
as a unit), and the resolved headline is owned in §4.4 and §10.

## 2. What a STEP is

Two levels, deliberately separated so verdict semantics never depend on
presentation grouping:

**The install EVENT (checkable unit).** The CPG assembly slice is a DAG of
events, one node kind, open-tagged like every other kind in this codebase:

| event | identity key (content, never ordinal — the INCR/FAB-Q3 lesson) | meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `place(part)` | the part's authored spec id (repeat instances carry their index, already threaded into provenance) | the part arrives at its final relative pose within its frame (§3.4) |
| `drive(connection, role_group)` | (connection label, role-group key) | the group's fasteners are driven — atomic; same-group siblings are co-driven in free order, exactly today's stack-exclusion semantics |
| `process(connection, kind)` | (connection label, kind) — v1 kind: `cure` | a non-geometric process fact; a valid ProcessStep kind that never reaches `fold` |
| `join(unit)` | the declared unit's name | a bench-assembled unit (a declared subassembly, or the whole detail under `bench_then_set`) enters the root frame at its final pose |

`bond(glued connection)` is `drive` with an empty hardware set — the event
exists (the glue-up is a real installation act) even though no fastener does.

**The reader STEP (presentation unit).** A deterministic grouping of
consecutive events in the canonical linearization (§5.1). Checks bind to
events and the partial order; the document binds to reader steps. Regrouping
the presentation can never change a verdict.

## 3. The order: derived where the model knows, declared with provenance where it doesn't

The global order is a **hybrid partial order** — a merge of edge families,
every edge provenance-stamped, cycle-checked as one graph (extending
`_check_install_order` from part-nodes to event-nodes):

### 3.1 The four families

1. **`technique_default` (lifted, exists today).** Each ConnectionType's
   `installed_before` part edges lift to event edges (part → its `place`
   event, hardware → its group's `drive` event). **Lift rule (review F-5): an
   edge whose two endpoints map to the SAME event node is order-vacuous and
   is dropped** — it expresses intra-event assembly order that the event's
   atomicity already owns. The pinned example: the platform's 40 intra-stack
   bolt→washer→washer→nut edges all collapse into their single
   `drive(conn, bolt_stack)` event; without the drop rule every bolted detail
   would fail at load, day one, on self-loop "cycles."

   **Multi-stack mapping rule (review R-2).** "Hardware → its group's drive
   event" assumes one group; hardware riding MULTIPLE role groups' stacks
   maps to the drive event its OWN type edges place it at-or-before. The
   pinned example is the FaceMountHanger's hanger piece, which rides BOTH
   screw groups' stacks (axes report §GEO-F1 — each group drives through one
   of its flanges): its `hanger → header screws` edge names the HEADER-side
   drive, so the hanger maps there, and the collapse is consistent —
   `hanger → header screws` becomes a self-loop and drops under the
   same-event rule, `hanger → hung` lands as a duplicate of the existing
   header-screws-before-hung technique edge, and `header → hanger` becomes
   place-before-drive. The alternative mapping is the pinned counterexample:
   mapped to the HUNG-side drive, `hanger → hung` lifts to
   `drive(hung-side) → place(hung)`, which cycles with
   `place(hung) → drive(hung-side)` (the type's own hung-screws edge /
   structural necessity) — every hanger-bearing detail, the platform first,
   would fail at load. Same defect class as the same-event rule: an order
   fact the lift must encode, found by walking the shipped edges.
2. **`structural_necessity` (derived, new).** A member must exist before its
   own connection's fasteners are driven: `place(member) → drive(conn, g)`
   for each member of `conn` — EXCEPT where a technique edge orders the
   opposite way (the hanger hangs its header screws before the hung member
   arrives; connection.py:1211 declares exactly that). Rule: the default edge
   is emitted only when no declared path contradicts it; a residual
   contradiction is a cycle and fails loudly. Likewise derived:
   `drive(bond) → process(cure)` for every glued connection — a bond precedes
   its own cure by the meaning of the words. Note the direction: every
   derived edge points INTO a drive/process event (something must exist
   first) — no derived family ever places an occupant AFTER a fastener,
   which is why §4.3's rung ceiling is what it is.
3. **`authored_sequence` (declared, new authoring surface).** A spec-level
   `sequence:` block: named stages, each listing connections/parts, stages
   totally ordered among themselves; plus point constraints
   (`after: [cure(<connection>)]` on a connection). This is where the caddy
   says *side screws after the rail-glue cure* and the platform says *toe
   screws before the leg bolts* (§4.4 — a why backed by the ToeScrewed wedge
   fact, not a coin flip). Every entry is REPRESENTED-rung authored content
   and requires a `why:` (the authored-embedment-override precedent — an
   order claim ships with its defense).
4. **`staging` (declared, new — §3.4).** Subassembly declarations and the
   `bench_then_set` whole-detail sugar: which parts are assembled apart, and
   when their unit joins the root frame. Context-body presence semantics live
   here too.

**Consistency checking.** One cycle check over the merged event graph (the
existing algorithm, teaching-error message naming the conflicting edges AND
their provenance families — a cycle between an authored stage and a technique
edge tells the author which claim to fix). A `sequence:` entry naming an
unknown connection/part is a loud load-time error (the existing unknown-key
registry discipline). A jointly-impossible order demand — the frame's mutual
screw-pair blocking is the live case (review Q1/F-1) — surfaces as an
unsatisfiable-order cycle, never as a quiet double PASS.

### 3.2 Retire / repeat / site

EXPECT/RETIRE retiring a connection retires its `drive`/`process` events and
every verdict they carry (the CL-3 closure precedent: retiring a connection
already retires its bolt's two axis verdicts). `repeat` instances get
per-index event identity for free (authored id + index). Site composition is
**deferred honestly**: v1 builds one CPG per fragment; a `bind:`-shared member
has one `place` event per owning fragment's graph and NO cross-fragment order
exists. The composed site's rod-vs-rung finding stays UNKNOWN with a reason
that names BOTH missing mechanisms (review F-4): it is a cross-fragment ORDER
question (the anchor plainly precedes the platform — a CPG v2 site graph
would prove it) AND an insertion-travel question (the rod's "corridor" is its
own body's insertion path — P1). Naming only one half would mislabel the
other.

### 3.4 Staging: subassemblies, frames, and context bodies (reshaped by review F-1/F-2)

The reviewer compiled the sit_reach_frame and proved the original
detail-level-only staging could not deliver its corpus row: the frame's eight
rail-screw corridors are blocked SYMMETRICALLY (each +X screw's corridor
crosses the ~2 in interior gap into the −X side's legs, rail, and mirror
screws — and vice versa), so clearing all eight demands each drive precede its
mirror, a direct 2-cycle; no flat sequence clears them, and the best in-situ
outcome is 4 PASS + 4 honest FAILs. The real build is different in kind:
**each side is benched as a unit** (legs + rail, screws driven flat with the
opposite side absent), then the two units are joined under the top plate
(whose cap-screw corridors PASS in final geometry — reviewer-verified). Per
the controller ruling, per-subassembly staging therefore ENTERS v1 scope,
specified minimally:

**The declaration.** In the `sequence:` block:

```yaml
sequence:
  subassemblies:
    - name: side_px
      parts: [front leg +X, back leg +X, side rail +X]
      why: "each side unit is screwed flat on the bench; the opposite
            side is not present"
```

A part belongs to at most ONE subassembly (loud error otherwise); nesting is
deliberately unsupported in v1. A connection whose members all lie inside one
subassembly is **bench-scoped** to it; a connection spanning a subassembly
boundary is root-scoped, and structural necessity derives
`join(unit) → drive(...)` for it (the frame's cap screws — driven only after
both sides exist in the root frame). `assembly: bench_then_set` is SUGAR for
one subassembly containing every non-context part (its `join` is the old
set_in_place) — one staging model, not two. `assembly: in_situ` is now an
EXPLICIT declaration (context semantics below); it is no longer the default.

**Frames and presence.** Every event carries a frame: a subassembly name, or
`root`. Bench-scoped place/drive events happen in their unit's frame;
everything else (including every `join`) is root. Presence of occupant p at
fastener f's drive event, tri-state per §4.1 throughout:

- f in frame S: only S's parts can be present (the bench holds only S);
  p ∈ S is judged by the graph restricted to S's events; every non-S part —
  including every context body — is absent, by the meaning of "assembled
  apart."
- f in root: a root part is judged via its `place` event; a subassembly part
  via its unit's `join` event; a context body per the staging declaration —
  `in_situ` places it before everything (provably-before ⇒ present),
  `bench_then_set` at the whole-detail join, and **UNDECLARED = UNORDERED**:
  no order fact relates it to anything, so a context-blocked corridor stays a
  blocking UNKNOWN naming the missing declaration (review F-2, controller
  ruling). This preserves today's truthful state for every author who
  declares nothing — the caddy's 8 stay UNKNOWN until the staging claim is
  authored — and makes explicit `in_situ` the honest mirror: declaring the
  caddy built ON the sofa flips the 8 to FAIL, which is the true on-the-sofa
  answer.

**Bench events precede their join — a derived staging-family rule (review
R-1):** `<every bench-scoped place/drive/process event of unit S> → join(S)`.
This encodes the declaration's own content — "assembled apart" means the
unit's internal work happens BEFORE the unit is set in place — as edges, not
as an unstated reading of the frame rule. Without it, valid linearizations
exist that join the unit first and drive its screws in the root frame
(mirror side present — exactly the corridor the bench clear claimed absent),
which would falsify §4.1's "a PASS holds for EVERY valid build order" for
bench clears, and Kahn's canonical linearization could print "set the unit
in place" before the unit's own screws. This rule is also what makes CAT-H's
step run (bench side +X → bench side −X → join under the top plate)
DERIVABLE rather than assumed.

**What a staging claim is, epistemically (review F-6).** A subassembly
declaration (and `bench_then_set`) is a REPRESENTED-rung authored claim with a
required `why:`. Its falsification channels in v1 are exactly the checks that
exist: connection MEMBERSHIP (a context body or cross-unit part that a
bench-scoped drive structurally needs closes a cycle) — and nothing else.
`bears_on` is not an edge family and does not reach the event graph, so **a
staging claim about connection-free context bodies is declared trust** (the
caddy's sofa arm is a member of zero connections — reviewer-verified — so its
staging claim is checkable by nothing today). This is carried per-verdict by
the rung wording (§4.3) and named as a residual (§10); real falsification of
staging is insertability (P1) territory.

## 4. Axis-3 activation: the UNKNOWN → PASS/FAIL split, by mechanism

### 4.1 Verdict semantics on the partial order (no linearization dependence)

For fastener f (event `e_f`) and corridor occupant p, judged on the merged
event graph with §3.4's frame/presence rules supplying p's governing event
`e_p` (place, join, or none):

- **`e_p` provably precedes-or-equals `e_f`** → p IS present when f is driven
  → **FAIL**, naming p, its owning connections, and the order facts (with
  provenance) that put it there. This is CAT-F's deferred mirror half.
- **`e_f` provably precedes `e_p`** → p provably absent → clear, disclosed:
  "p occupies the corridor in final geometry but arrives later (order:
  <edges + provenance>)" — the existing own-connection `_later_note` wording
  generalized to the global graph.
- **Neither path exists** (including a context body with no staging
  declaration) → **UNKNOWN — build order underdetermined**, naming p AND the
  missing constraint ("no order relates <e_f> and <e_p>; a `sequence:` entry,
  staging declaration, or technique edge would resolve it"). The UNKNOWN
  teaches its own fix.

This deliberately does NOT pick a linearization: a PASS holds for EVERY valid
build order, so the doc's presented sequence (§5) is pure presentation. A
builder who deviates from the printed order but respects the constraints is
still covered by the verdicts — that is the claim's honest shape.

**The delta from today's party rule, stated (review F-7).** `_classify` today
FAILs any same-connection occupant not explicitly ordered after the fastener
("party present at this joint's own install step"). Under §4.1 there is no
party-presence assumption: a same-connection MEMBER occupant keeps its FAIL
via structural necessity (its place event provably precedes the drive), but
same-connection HARDWARE in a different role group with no order path either
way becomes **UNKNOWN — build order underdetermined** where today it FAILs.
This is a deliberate honesty upgrade, not a quiet weakening: the old rule
ASSUMED presence; the new one reports that the model does not know, and names
the fix. No shipped detail changes verdict under it (the hanger's two groups
are ordered by its own technique edges; bolt stacks are one group —
reviewer-verified), but the synthetic CAT-E-lineage pins in
test_install_axes.py DO hit it: the implementation re-pins the
unordered-party FAIL fixtures to the new truth (member cases keep FAIL with
the structural-necessity provenance printed; the cross-group-hardware case
re-pins to the named UNKNOWN) — re-derived deliberately, never silently
weakened.

### 4.2 Mechanics: reclassification, not resweeping

Axis 2 already intersects each fastener's corridor with every part
individually and classifies the hits (`_classify`). Axis 3 is the SAME hit
sets **reclassified against the event graph** instead of the own-connection
closure — under the assumption, stated and disclosed, that **parts accrete at
their final relative pose** (no temporary positions; true of every shipped
detail; temporary poses/supports are CPG v2). Under that assumption the
partial assembly at any step is a subset of final-pose solids, so
presence-filtered final hits EQUAL partial-assembly hits — reclassification
is exact, not an approximation (reviewer-confirmed, review Q3, including the
bench-frame case: within a unit parts sit at final relative pose and absent
context contributes no hits). So axis 3 costs graph reachability, not new
geometry sweeps. The termination axis (axis 1) is unaffected — a shank path
is a final-geometry fact. `_Scope.installed_after`'s own-connection closure
becomes a projection of the one event graph so there is one order truth, not
two (single-source discipline).

### 4.3 What rung each verdict claims (the ladder is binding)

`REPRESENTED < GEOMETRY-PROVEN < SEQUENCE-PROVEN`, and the house precedent
stands: a declared order never yields a bare top-rung claim. The review
closed Q4 by tracing the derived families (F-8): every derived edge points
INTO a drive/process event, so **no derived fact can ever place an occupant
provably AFTER a fastener** — every clear's deciding path necessarily
includes at least one declared fact (technique_default, authored_sequence, or
staging). Therefore, as a v1 scope line:

- **v1 claims SEQUENCE-PROVEN nowhere.** Every axis-3 clear is worded
  **"geometry proven at the DECLARED build order"** with every deciding
  declaration + provenance printed inline, explicitly "declared, not
  sequence-proven" — the exact generalization of today's ordered-after stamp.
  It resolves the blocking UNKNOWN (the builder has a checked, defensible
  order on paper) without claiming a rung the mechanism didn't earn.
- Axis-3 FAILs may rest on purely derived facts (structural necessity is
  enough to prove a blocker present); FAILs don't wear rungs — their evidence
  provenance prints like any finding's.
- The SEQUENCE-PROVEN rung is RESERVED for a future mechanism that derives
  after-direction order from geometric necessity (insertability-class
  derivation); until one exists, nothing claims it.
- Insertability is not analyzed at ANY rung in v1; every staging-leaning
  verdict says so (§6).

## 5. The reader-facing steps (the owner's actual ask)

### 5.1 Canonical linearization + grouping

One deterministic linearization of the event graph (Kahn's algorithm; ties
broken by declared stage/subassembly declaration order, then connection
declaration order, then part authored id — byte-stable docs, the house rule).
Reader steps group consecutive events: one step per declared stage or
subassembly where they exist (a benched unit's internal events present as
that unit's step run, its `join` a visible "set the unit in place" moment);
within a stage (or absent stages) one step per connection install unit, with
`place` events folded into the step of the first connection that consumes the
part; `process(cure)` is always its OWN step (the owner's "step 2: glue"
instinct — a cure is a stop-and-wait the builder must see). The grouping rule
is a pure function of the graph; regrouping cannot change verdicts (§2).
Per-connection default grouping was adversarially sized (review Q6): the
worst stage-less corpus case is the trebuchet at ~13 steps (its connections,
not its 57 parts — parts fold into their consuming connection's step), a page
section, not an explosion; arrival-cluster grouping is deliberately NOT built
(a derived heuristic whose churn under small edits would re-key step renders
for zero verdict value — revisit only if a real detail ships unreadable).

### 5.2 What each step's text derives from (nothing hand-typed)

- **Parts placed**: names, BOM labels, and fabrication notes read from the
  ONE source the cut plan reads (`ProcessRecord.fab_note`) — the viewer
  tooltip precedent.
- **Fasteners driven**: the resolved contract — method, entry face, tool
  envelope, embedment — with per-field provenance, the same
  render_install_disclosures_md content scoped to the step.
- **Process facts**: the cure step's text derives from the glued connection's
  declared process assumptions (glue both faces, clamp, cure per label) with
  the WHY; when the `sequence:` block pins screws after cure, that constraint
  prints on BOTH steps ("do not drive the side screws until this cure
  completes — order: authored, why: …").
- **Declared order visibility**: any step whose position rests on an
  authored_sequence/staging claim carries the claim and its why inline. A
  reader can distinguish "this order is what the joints require" from "this
  order is what the author chose" — declared order is visible, never ambient.

### 5.3 Per-step views

Two surfaces, one source:

- **Viewer (primary, near-free)**: the GLB already carries every part as a
  named node; `build_viewer_payload` gains a per-part step index, and the
  viewer gets a step slider that shows parts with step ≤ k, highlighting
  step-k arrivals. No new geometry export; one payload field + viewer JS.
- **Static PNGs (print surface)**: a partial-assembly render per reader step

## 6. Honesty rules

1. **A declared order is a construction-sequence CLAIM.** The install.py
   never-add-edges rule generalizes verbatim: **never declare a sequence
   entry, stage, subassembly, or staging mode to silence a verdict.** Every
   declared order fact carries provenance + an authored `why:`; every verdict
   that leans on one prints it; and the checks falsify what they can — a
   declared order that puts a blocker before a fastener flips that fastener
   to FAIL (§4.1), a declared order contradicting a technique edge or
   structural necessity is a load-time cycle error naming both claims, and a
   jointly-impossible demand (the frame's mutual pairs) is unsatisfiable, not
   quietly cleared. **What v1 cannot falsify is stated, not implied away
   (review F-6):** a staging claim about connection-free context bodies has
   no checking mechanism today — connection membership is the only falsifier,
   and the caddy's sofa arm has none — so such claims are declared trust,
   carried by the rung wording and the §10 residual, pending insertability.
2. **Underdetermined ≠ resolved.** Where the partial order doesn't relate a
   blocker to a fastener — including every context body with no staging
   declaration — the verdict is UNKNOWN naming the gap; never a default
   assumption in either direction.
3. **Insertability (P1) is out of scope for v1, stated per-verdict.** Axis 3
   v1 proves TOOL-CORRIDOR access at each step; it does not prove a part (or
   a benched unit at its join) can physically travel to its pose. Every
   staging-dependent PASS, the platform's joist-between-bolts sentence, and
   the site rod-vs-rung finding say so in their sentence, not in a footnote.
4. **Rung discipline** per §4.3: v1 claims SEQUENCE-PROVEN nowhere; every
   clear is "geometry proven at the DECLARED build order," deciding
   declarations named.
5. **The step doc contains no hand-typed sequence prose — including the
   prose that already exists (review F-10).** The caddy panel's authored
   fieldnote ("Hidden rail joints — glue, then screws, all off the sofa") and
   buy-lede sequence phrasing in `scripts/single_detail_report.py` are
   pre-existing violations of the single-source mandate the moment derived
   steps ship beside them: they DERIVE (the fieldnote's content becomes a
   render of the caddy's own step run) or RETIRE in the same arc. CAT-K's
   grep-closer is required to trip on them — no quiet exemption boundary.

## 10. What this deliberately does NOT do

- **No insertability** (P1): tool corridors at steps, not part travel paths;
  said in every verdict that would otherwise overclaim.
- **No real falsification of connection-free staging claims** (review F-6):
  the caddy's bench claim, and any staging of context bodies that participate
  in no connection, is declared trust — checkable by nothing until
  insertability. The rung wording carries it on every verdict; this line
  names it.
- **No schedules, durations, crew, or tooling logistics** — order only; cure
  is a precedence fact, not a clock.
- **No temporary poses or supports** — parts accrete at final relative pose
  (within their frame), disclosed as an assumption of every axis-3 verdict
  class.
- **No site-level cross-fragment order** — per-fragment graphs; the composed
  site's rod-vs-rung stays UNKNOWN naming both its missing mechanisms.
- **No silent reclassification.** The three corpus docs regenerate with their
  new truthful states and full disclosures. The default-staging choice (§3.4)
  is the specific guard: undeclared context stays UNORDERED, so today's
  UNKNOWNs remain UNKNOWNs (re-worded to name the missing declaration instead
  of the absent CPG) for any author who declares nothing — no UNKNOWN→FAIL
  flip happens without an explicit `in_situ` claim. The §4.1 party-rule delta
  (unordered cross-group hardware: FAIL → named UNKNOWN) is stated, defended,
  and re-pinned — the one place a today-FAIL softens, and it softens to an
  honest question with its fix named.
- **The headline, owned:** v1 flips all 18 standing install-order UNKNOWNs —
  caddy 8 and frame 8 on DECLARED staging claims (the caddy's on declared
  trust per the line above; the frame's partially falsifiable through its
  cross-unit cap-screw necessity edges), platform 2 on an authored order
  defended by the type's own wedge fact. Zero of the 18 claim SEQUENCE-PROVEN;
  all 18 read "geometry proven at the DECLARED build order" with their
  deciding claims on paper. What becomes impossible is (a) shipping a
  sequence-dependent access question as anything but a named verdict, (b) a
  declared order silencing a check rather than being tested by it, and (c)
  step prose drifting from the model, because there is no authored step
  prose. What does NOT disappear: a wrong-but-cycle-free declared staging on
  connection-free context that honest checks cannot yet falsify — it ships as
  a visible, provenance-stamped claim, which is the honest v1 ceiling.

**Verdict:** BUILDABLE AS A PROTOTYPE, BUT NOT WITHOUT GUESSING; not a
consumer assembly manual and not release-ready for unattended hot-drink use.

## What the document succeeds at

- All five fabricated members, their actual sizes, the rail placement, cup
  center, and all eight screw stations are identifiable.
- The top-length fit formula, dry-fit gate, chair/cushion check, cup-fit
  criteria, tool choices, PPE, and uncertainty disclosures are unusually
  strong for a DIY plan.
- The hidden-fastener geometry and the registration rails' purpose are
  understandable.

## Safety / analysis boundary

Structural capacity, support/stability, sliding resistance, and functional
hot-drink use remain honestly NOT ANALYZED. The reviewer would build only a
fit-and-stability prototype and would not infer a safe working load. This is
an analysis limitation, not an instruction-comprehension defect.

## Instruction-comprehension findings carried into +presentation

The technical document remains machine-facing. Compared with official IKEA
LACK, IKEA SEKTION, and Whalen Marvale manuals, it needs a separate concise
manual with:

- labeled/exploded parts and exact hardware inventory;
- one-action numbered panels and per-step part/hardware/tool callouts;
- a human sequence covering measure, cut, bore, edge treatment/sanding,
  dry-fit, glue/clamp, cure, side fastening, finishing, and final test;
- practical cup-template and bore-rim treatment guidance;
- exact product-selection fields for screws, adhesive, clamps, and finish;
- a visible hard break at cure and explicit final fit/stability/use gates.

The review also noted rail/cup formulas, tolerances, finish cure, hot/spill
protection, and purchase efficiency as future content or design decisions.
They must not be fabricated by the presentation renderer when the semantic
model does not supply them.

Design brief

Use
Provide a removable hard surface for a cup and small personal items on a padded armchair arm.
Loads
Carry its own weight plus an ordinary filled mug and small accessories without relying on permanent sofa attachment.
Fit range
Preserve the existing parameterized fit around a six-inch arm with clearance for upholstery and removal by hand.
Appearance
Read as compact indoor furniture with clean exterior faces, eased edges, and no exposed utilitarian fasteners.
Builder skill
Suit an intermediate home woodworker while making any accuracy-sensitive joint setup explicit in the instructions.
Tools
tape measure and square, table saw or accurate miter saw, drill or drill press, clamps, sanding tools

Required features

  • req_service_surface — Provide a stable top surface that transfers ordinary service loads into both hanging side panels.
  • req_fit — Fit over the modeled arm and preserve clearance from adjacent upholstery throughout installation and use.
  • req_registration — Limit cross-arm rocking without gripping, fastening to, or otherwise modifying the upholstered chair arm.
  • req_cup_opening — Retain the modeled centered cup opening and its stated loose-fit verification before any production redesign.
  • req_removable — Lift off by hand and remain compact enough to store when the chair arm should be unobstructed.

Prioritized constraints

  1. constraint_simplicity — Minimize permanent part count and repeated assembly operations before optimizing secondary construction preferences.
  2. constraint_fit_preservation — Avoid consuming the interior fit envelope with reinforcement unless a required function justifies that material.
  3. constraint_appearance — Keep metal fasteners and coarse structural seams away from the visible top and outside faces.
  4. constraint_tools — Prefer methods achievable with the listed home-shop tools and clearly disclose specialized jigs or setups.
  5. constraint_instruction_clarity — Prefer repeated joints and short verification sequences that can be explained without hidden positioning assumptions.

Simplification review

  • exterior structural panels: Form the usable top and two outside faces while carrying service loads around the chair arm. Joinery review: These three visible panels are irreducible, although their corner connections can be integrated directly into their edges.
  • full-depth registration rails: Reinforce both butt joints and present deep interior faces that limit cross-arm rocking at the specified reveal. Joinery review: Reinforced miters, shouldered rabbets, or compact concealed hardware could absorb the corner function while side geometry handles fit.
  • concealed structural screws: Clamp each rail to a side panel and resist joint separation through face-grain screw engagement. Joinery review: Dowels or purpose-made corner joinery could replace these eight metal fasteners and their drilling operations.

Decision

Selected concept: reinforced_miter — Three-panel reinforced-miter shell

Application: implemented

The reinforced-miter shell best combines documented corner strength, conventional three-panel topology, preserved fit volume, low material use, and finished-furniture appearance.

Decisive cells: reinforced_miter.strength, reinforced_miter.part_count, reinforced_miter.material, reinforced_miter.appearance, reinforced_miter.instruction_complexity

Accepted tradeoffs

  • Accurate miter cutting and diagonal dowel layout demand more setup care than square-cut hardware assembly.
  • A future production redesign must prove cross-arm registration without assuming the current rails remain necessary.
